Principles

  • Learning vs winning the system

    • Since the day we are born learning starts and its a lifelong process. To be an efficient learner, the focus must shift from winning a system to actual long term learning. Here is the great article that describe it best. The Lesson to Unlearn

  • What is success?

    • Drive and persistance leads to success. To succeed we need to take risks and make mistakes. Those who are successful today are the risk takers and efficient learners. Hard work is essential for success but if you know the science and technologies of efficient learning you don't have to work hard.

  • The Obstacles

    • Most people won't take risk because of the obstacles. What you do when you encounter the obstacle will define your future success. When you feel like you suck, you found something people aren't willing to do. So get comfortable with not knowing and be curious. Obstacles are the reasons that makes your skill valuable. The Obstacle Is the Way

  • The Dip

    • Dip is knowing what isn't working for you and being able to follow what works for you. There is a possibility that you are not simply cut out for the path ahead. It is just as important to know when to quit, to turnaround and do something different as it is to push to go over the obstacles. A great book on dip.

  • Failures don't count

    • Everybody that you met today experiences you as you right now. Nobody care about test you failed, mistakes you made in the past. Your mistakes don't count against you. Every failure brings you closer to where you want to be.

  • It's all in the frame

    • Frame are what human use to change perspective. Almost everything in this universe have different context or frames that you can use to view thing in different way. Framing is moving the negative ideas where we don't have control over our future into looking out from different perspective having a begineer mindset. Framing effect

  • Pareto Principle

    • The Pareto principle (also known as the 80/20 rule, the law of the vital few, or the principle of factor sparsity) states that, for many events, roughly 80% of the effects come from 20% of the causes. Learn More

  • Skill stacking

    • Working on one skill is good but you have to understand that there is someone out there working twice as hard as you on same skill. Instead of getting good at one thing, be good at multiple things and combine them. stack your skills in different unique way, that is unique to you. To really be a master at something it requires a lifetime of dedication. There is always going to be somebody working harder than you do. With skill stacking its actually more effective to be in top 10% or 20% in serveral different skills that you can stack together that is completely unique to you so that you can be in the 5% of that skill. Mastery (book)

  • Happiness Factors

    • The key to find all your happiness is learning and inorder to be an efficient learner you need to be happy. If you are not in the good state of mind you are automatically in disadvantage. Look at your everyday life and think about the key factors that make you happy.

  • Your productivity time

    • Figure out your productive time and structure your learning at that time. The key is that there is no-one better than other. Every person is different and our job is to figure out our productivity time. It could be in the morning or evening anytime that works for you.

  • Self-learning paradigm

    • The core of self-learning is the idea of personal autonomy. Nobody is going to tell you to learn the topic to develop the skill to develop further and further each day. That is the choice you have to make.

Key Takeaway:

Success is the path and learning is the key. Understand the obstacle, dip, compound learning, skill stacking with a begineer mindset and find happiness factors.

Techniqes

  • Pomodoro technique

    • The Pomodoro Technique is a time management method developed by Francesco Cirillo in the late 1980s. The technique uses a timer to break down work into intervals, traditionally 25 minutes in length, separated by short breaks. Each interval is known as a pomodoro, from the Italian word for 'tomato', after the tomato-shaped kitchen timer that Cirillo used as a university student. Learn more

  • Chunk the subject

    • Chunking is the idea of mental leap that helps us to unite different bits of knowledge into something meaningful or give us context in life. When we focus on something, we create chunk of knowledge. When we learn something we are creating neurons patterns and connecting them with pre-existing patterns in our long-term memory. Breaking things down into basic fundamental truth and start there. You can adopt bottom-up learning which starting small and connect the dot or Top-down learning which is to see the big picture in first.

  • Spaced repetition

    • Revisiting and practicing what you learned is important because it is the best way to build synaptic connection in our neurons. It enhances memory, problem solving, and transfer of learning to new contexts. After a certain point the neuron connections get strong enough that we don't have to repeat as often, only once in a while. We also have to remember about the forgetting curve. Learn More

  • Delibrate practice

    • It is doing the things that are hard. We avoid the easy stuff and we want to take on the challenging projects. The ways to have Delibrate practice inhabited are;

    1. carve out a period of distracted focus.
    2. During that focus period push yourself to the edge of your ability.
    3. Always seek immediate feedback or mentorship
    4. Write down your progress

    Every time you feel that feeling of giving up or you get butterflies in stomach. That's when you know you're in that zone of comfort and discomfort. You want to be right at the edge because that's how we push past our boundaries and get better and better. Best book to understand delibrate practice

  • create a roadmap

    • You want to know what path you need to know inorder to get where you want to be. Figure out what topics you plan to study, your objective in this path is to gain enough knowledge about the learning path that you have to be able to recognise. By having a plan you need to learn. When creating a roadmap focus on what topic is most important. My favourite tool to create a roadmap

  • Interleaving

    • Interleaving is a technique to use different problems and sessions to learn something. Interleaving is a very powerful concept. It is the reason as we age, we tend to become smarter. We are able to interleave different experiences in our lives and all sorts of knowledge to connect different ideas. The goal is to "mix it up". How am I going to mix up my learning?

  • Einstellung effect

    • Einstellung is the development of mechanised state of mind. It is the ability to solve a given problem in a specific manner even though better or more appropriate methods of solving the problem exists. Einstellung is not having the rigid mindset. Intergrative complexity is the idea of willingness to accept multiple perspectives which helps to overcome einstellung. When learning don't stick with one resource, ask yourself what is option C and always understand there's pros and cons to everything. Read More

  • Importance of Community

    • In community we can learn from others and get immediate feedback when working or learning something. You can overcome obstacles and you can get motivated because others are doing the same thing we are. Most of all we can have accountability that is how we can make sure that we are being accoutable.

  • Habits

    • Habits are energy savers of our brain. As in James Clear book Atomic habits there is four step of habits:-

    1. Find a cue which means something that triggers us and puts us in that habit mode.
    2. Make a routine and follow it constantly. It is what brain does to respond to cue.
    3. Reward yourself along the way. Habit should make us feel good.
    4. Belive: Habit have the power because we actually believe in them so believe.

    Don't break the chain is the best technique to implement habits. You just have to do it over and over and over until it become a habit.

Key Takeaway

It's totally upto us to apply whichever technique fits best to us. The concept of the technique should be clearly understood and stick with it to make a habit which in long term saves us a lot of energy.
